BACKGROUND:
Upon completion of the wafer fabrication process routing for SCL-555 Lot 2 (LOT-2026-02), the Electrical Wafer Sort (EWS) returned a final yield average of 45.8%. This represents a critical yield excursion event, falling significantly below the 90.0% minimum quality threshold. An urgent failure analysis and root cause investigation was initiated.

FAILURE ANALYSIS & METROLOGY FINDINGS:

1. Scanning Electron Microscopy (SEM) and Focused Ion Beam (FIB) Diagnostics
The Failure Analysis (FA) team selected 5 failing dies from different wafers (Wafer #03, #07, #12, #18, #22) representing center and edge layout locations.
- Under SEM examination, extensive aluminum-copper metal bridging shorts were detected between adjacent lines on the Metal 1 layer.
- FIB cross-sections revealed that the trench profiles between Metal 1 interconnect lines had a vertical thickness of 5500 Angstroms, but the bottoms of these trenches were not fully cleared. A residual metal layer ranging from 400 to 1200 Angstroms thick remained in the spacing gaps, directly shorting adjacent signals.
- Spacing measurements between the Metal 1 lines at ADI (After Develop Inspection) were verified at 0.18 um +/- 0.01 um, confirming that the lithography stepper printed the shrunk layout dimensions correctly.

2. Fabrication Process Log Audit
The investigation team performed a detailed audit of the manufacturing logs for LOT-2026-02 in the Fabrication Operations database.
- The logs show that when LOT-2026-02 reached the Metal 1 dry-etch step, the operators ran the lot using the standard baseline recipe CL_ETCH_M1_0.25 on the Dry-Etch-04 chamber.
- This recipe (CL_ETCH_M1_0.25) employs a total etch time of 120 seconds, which was developed and optimized for the 0.25 um spacing rule layout (Lot 1).
- Because the layout spacing of Lot 2 was shrunk to 0.18 um, the aspect ratio of the gaps increased. This reduction in width restricts the diffusion of chlorine reactive radicals ($Cl*$) into the narrow trenches and impedes the out-diffusion of volatile etch by-products ($AlCl_3$). This phenomenon is known as RIE lag or micro-loading.
- As a direct consequence of the micro-loading effect, the etch rate inside the 0.18 um spaces was reduced by approximately 22% compared to the open fields. Running the legacy recipe CL_ETCH_M1_0.25 resulted in under-etching of these narrow spaces, leaving behind the residual metal bridging observed in the SEM and FIB analysis.

CORRECTIVE ACTIONS & DISPOSITION:
1. The 45.8% Wafer Sort Yield is confirmed to be caused by Metal 1 bridging shorts due to dry-etch under-clearance under RIE lag.
2. The legacy dry-etch recipe CL_ETCH_M1_0.25 must not be used for layouts with features under 0.25 um.
3. For all future 0.18 um spacing layout runs, the etching recipe must be updated to CL_ETCH_M1_0.18 on the Dry-Etch-04 chamber. This new recipe extends the etch time by 20% (to 144 seconds) and adjusts the Cl2/BCl3 ratio to enhance the vertical chemical etching component.
4. Lot 2 (LOT-2026-02) is rejected for delivery and quarantined in the QA hold locker.
