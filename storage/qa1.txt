QUALITY REVIEW AND COMMERCIAL RELIABILITY CLEARANCE REPORT: LOT 1
This report details the final reliability stresses and qualification results performed on the SCL-555 Lot 1 packages.

1. QUALIFICATION WORKFLOW AND STRESS LEVELS
To clear the SCL-555 MCU for automotive-grade applications, Lot 1 sample packages were subjected to three primary environmental stresses in accordance with AEC-Q100 standards.

2. ENVIRONMENTAL STRESS TESTS
- High-Temperature Operational Life (HTOL):
  - Sample Size: 100 units.
  - Stress Parameters: 1000 hours at 125C with dynamic clock signals and 2.75V supply voltage (10% over-voltage stress).
  - Parametric checks: Completed at 168h, 500h, and 1000h.
  - Result: 0 failures, 0 parametric drift shifts observed.
- Temperature Cycling (TC):
  - Sample Size: 100 units.
  - Stress Parameters: 500 cycles from -40C to 125C, air-to-air chamber, 15-minute dwell time.
  - Diagnostics: CSAM scan at 500 cycles.
  - Result: 0 delamination failures, 0 package cracking.
- Highly Accelerated Stress Test (HAST):
  - Sample Size: 50 units.
  - Stress Parameters: 96 hours at 130C and 85% relative humidity under 2.5V static bias.
  - Result: 0 leakage excursions, 0 corrosion events.

3. ELECTRICAL METROLOGY AUDIT
Post-stress electrical measurements show:
- Standby leakage current (Idd_standby): Mean of 1.4 uA (Limit: < 10 uA).
- Output High Voltage (Voh): Mean of 2.42V at 4mA load (Limit: > 2.2V).
- Threshold Voltage (Vth) parametric shift: < 15 mV.

DISPOSITION:
Lot 1 (LOT-2026-01) has successfully passed all AEC-Q100 equivalent reliability qualification stress tests. No gate oxide breakdown, parameter shift, or packaging integrity loss occurred. The lot is formally cleared for commercial release and shipment to the customer.
